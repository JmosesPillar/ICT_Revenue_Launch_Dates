What This Data Suggests

Key takeaways from the combined GA4 revenue + launch date analysis.
Launch days do outperform — but not always

Average revenue on a launch day is $131,954 vs. $72,709 on a non‑launch day — nearly double. Across 47 launches with reliable data, 57% show a real lift the following week (avg +22.7%), but 43% are flat or negative. A go‑live date alone doesn't guarantee a bump.

Cruises outperform on average, but are more volatile

Cruise launches average +28.6% lift vs. +25.3% for tours — but the cruise median is just +0.8% vs. +10.9% for tours. A handful of huge cruise wins (e.g. +271%, +198%) are pulling the average up while most barely move.

Desktop converts ~5.7x better than mobile

Mobile drives 69% of sessions but converts at only 0.085%; desktop gets 28% of sessions but converts at 0.485%. That's why desktop accounts for 68.7% of total revenue — consistent with "research on phone, book on desktop."

Conversion rate is sliding as traffic grows

Sessions roughly doubled (~38K/mo in mid‑2025 → 70‑115K/mo by early 2026), but conversion fell from ~0.19% to as low as 0.08‑0.13%. Average order value crept up slightly ($6,968 → $7,489), so revenue is being propped up by fewer, bigger transactions.

Two months look like outliers

September 2025 ($2.47M) and January 2026 ($2.66M) are well above the typical $300K–$900K monthly range. Worth checking whether these reflect a large group booking, a promo, or a data/refund anomaly before treating them as "normal."

Wednesday is the strongest day, Saturday the weakest

Average daily revenue peaks on Wednesday ($103K) and is lowest on Saturday ($58K) — useful if launch timing or marketing sends can be scheduled around it.

Note: this shows correlation, not proof of causation — a launch landing the same week as a revenue bump doesn't rule out other things happening at the same time (paid campaigns, newsletters, seasonality). Use the Lift chart and Launch Detail table below to spot‑check any specific launch.
